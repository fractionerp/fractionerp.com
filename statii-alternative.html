Key Differences to Evaluate

Pricing and users

Fraction ERP plans are structured by capability tier. Statii publishes separate main-user and shop-floor-user pricing. The comparison is less about headline price and more about how many people need full system access versus shop floor access.

MRP focus vs wider ERP coverage

Statii's public positioning is strongly MRP and manufacturing-management led. Fraction ERP should be evaluated where the buyer also wants quality, SOP and training, calibration, traceability, reporting and commercial workflows in the same ERP environment.

Shop floor model

Both systems address shop floor use. Fraction ERP includes unlimited free shop floor data capture users; Statii publishes a separate shop floor user price. The right fit depends on how many operators need shop floor access and what they need to record.

Implementation fit

The safest way to compare implementation is to map your own process: quoting, BOMs, MRP, works orders, purchasing, shop floor updates, despatch and reporting. A demo should show those workflows using realistic examples.

When to Choose Each System

Choose Fraction ERP if you:

  • Need connected ERP coverage beyond MRP
  • Want unlimited free shop floor data capture users
  • Need quality, SOP, training or compliance workflows in the ERP
  • Prefer tiered capability-based pricing

Choose Statii if you:

  • Want focused MRP and job control for SME manufacturing
  • Like published main-user and shop-floor-user pricing
  • Are evaluating around production planning, stock, orders and purchasing
  • Want to compare an MRP-led system before looking at wider ERP coverage
