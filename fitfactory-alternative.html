Fraction ERP vs Fitfactory

A factual comparison for UK manufacturers

Fraction ERP and Fitfactory both serve manufacturing SMEs, but their current product structures are different. Fraction is a connected cloud ERP. Fitfactory presents a modular portfolio led by Tricorn MRP, with MES tools that can be selected separately or connected to an existing ERP or MRP system.

Scope note: Fitfactory also has a page for a new Fitfactory ERP that is labelled “Coming Soon”. We do not treat that announced product as currently available or assume what its commercial package will include.

Start With the Product Structure

This distinction affects implementation, integration and the scope you should ask each supplier to demonstrate.

Fraction ERP

One connected cloud ERP

Sales, purchasing, inventory, works orders, scheduling, shop-floor data, quality, shipping and invoicing share the same ERP records.

Capabilities expand by plan, with some specialist modules and integrations available separately.

Fitfactory

A modular manufacturing portfolio

Fitfactory describes Tricorn as its manufacturing MRP and production-control system, available in cloud or on-premise deployments.

Its MES offering combines Scheduler, Shop Floor Data Collection and Machine Monitoring; those modules can also work with an existing ERP or MRP through an API.

Reasons to Shortlist Each System

These are requirement-led reasons to investigate further, not a claim that either product will suit every manufacturer.

Shortlist Fraction ERP if you want to assess:

  • A single cloud ERP spanning office and production workflows
  • Published plan pricing and subscription inclusions
  • Native iOS and Android manufacturing workflows
  • SOP, task, quality, safety and workforce records connected to ERP data
  • A browser-based service without on-premise infrastructure

Shortlist Fitfactory if you want to assess:

  • On-premise as well as cloud deployment for Tricorn
  • A modular MES alongside an existing ERP or MRP
  • Direct machine-state monitoring using Fitfactory's sensor offering
  • What-if and resource-aware scheduling as a specific MES capability
  • Specialist Fitfactory offerings for aerospace or treatment processes

Questions to Put to Both Suppliers

Use the same script and sample job with both vendors. That produces a more useful comparison than a generic feature checklist.

1

Show the complete workflow

Demonstrate one of your real jobs from quote and material demand through production, delivery and actual cost.

2

Define the contracted scope

List every product, module, user type, integration and roadmap item. Separate what is available now from planned functionality.

3

Price the whole implementation

Request written costs for software, training, migration, integrations, hardware, support and any work needed after go-live.

4

Test production exceptions

Ask how the system handles shortages, scrap, rework, subcontract operations, late jobs and changing priorities.

5

Verify data ownership

Confirm backups, security, audit history, export formats and how you retrieve your records if you leave.

6

Put support in writing

Clarify support channels, service hours, response expectations, named responsibilities and what requires paid consultancy.

Fraction ERP and Fitfactory FAQs

Fitfactory is the supplier and wider product portfolio. Its current manufacturing production-control page presents Tricorn as the MRP system, alongside Fitfactory MES and other modules. A separately named Fitfactory ERP appears on a page labelled “Coming Soon”.

Fitfactory advertises Tricorn in both cloud and on-premise forms. Its MES modules are also presented as accessible across devices and able to integrate with an ERP or MRP.

Fitfactory advertises Machine Monitoring as a dedicated MES module. Its published description covers machine status, downtime reasons, alerts and performance history using connected sensors.

Fraction ERP provides production scheduling and shop-floor data collection itself. For direct machine data, it publishes manufacturing integrations including Intouch Monitoring rather than advertising its own machine sensor product.

This page does not guess. Fraction publishes current plan prices, while a meaningful Fitfactory comparison requires a proposal for the exact products, modules, deployment, training and integrations you need. Compare written total costs over the same period and scope.
