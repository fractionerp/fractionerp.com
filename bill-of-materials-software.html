What is a Bill of Materials (BOM)?

A Bill of Materials (BOM) is the structured list of all components, materials, and sub-assemblies required to manufacture a product.

It defines:

  • What you need to build a product
  • How those components relate to each other
  • The quantities required at each level

In manufacturing, the BOM is not just a list — it's the foundation of your entire production process.

Without accurate BOMs:

  • Production planning breaks down
  • Costs become unreliable
  • Material shortages increase
  • Engineering and operations fall out of sync

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a multi-level BOM?

A multi-level BOM includes sub-assemblies within a product, showing the full hierarchy of components required for manufacturing.

How do you manage BOM revisions?

Using controlled revision tracking, ensuring only approved versions are used in production.

How does a BOM affect costing?

The BOM defines all materials and quantities, forming the basis of accurate product costing.
