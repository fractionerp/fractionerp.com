## Why revision control matters (more than people think)

Most SOP systems fail for one simple reason: nobody trusts the document.

If the shop floor thinks the SOP is out of date, they'll ignore it. If supervisors aren't sure which version is current, they'll teach "their way". If changes aren't tracked, you can't learn from problems or prove that improvements were implemented.

Revision control solves that by making it clear:

- What the current standard is
- What changed since the last version
- Who approved it
- Who needs to be trained or re-confirmed

That's how you stop procedures becoming shelfware.

## Assignment and sign-off: turning documents into behaviour

Writing an SOP is the easy part. The hard part is getting consistent use.

The assignment feature in Fraction ERP is designed for exactly that. When you assign an SOP to a team member, you can ensure:

- The SOP is seen by the right people (not "available somewhere")
- Understanding is confirmed, not assumed
- Compliance becomes a managed process, not a hope

This is especially useful when you:

- Introduce a new process
- Change a method after a quality issue
- Bring in new equipment
- Onboard new starters
- Need to prove training and control for customer or certification requirements

## The general benefits of SOPs (when they're done properly)

SOPs aren't about bureaucracy. They're about removing avoidable problems and freeing people up to focus on real work.

Here's what well-managed SOPs typically deliver:

- **Consistency and repeatability:** the same job is done the same way, regardless of who's on shift
- **Better quality:** fewer defects caused by variation and "best guesses"
- **Faster onboarding:** new starters become productive sooner, with less reliance on tribal knowledge
- **Less firefighting:** fewer interruptions, fewer "how do I&hellip;?" questions, fewer avoidable mistakes
- **Improved safety and compliance:** critical steps aren't skipped because they're "obvious"
- **A foundation for continuous improvement:** once you have a standard, you can improve it &mdash; without a standard, you're just changing opinions

And importantly: SOPs reduce the risk that your business performance depends on one or two key people being present.

- **Daily management** (so issues are dealt with fast)
- **Continuous improvement** (so standards get better over time)
