If you're in manufacturing, engineering, or fabrication, you already know the cheapest way to grow is rarely chasing brand-new customers.

The fastest, most profitable growth often comes from the customers you already have &mdash; the ones who want to place more orders, as long as you make it easy to buy from you.

And in most B2B supply chains, "easy to buy from" boils down to two things:

- You deliver on time (consistently)
- You're clear and transparent when they ask, "Where's my order?"

That's exactly where Fraction ERP helps: it gives you the visibility and control to improve on-time delivery, and the tracking tools to answer customer questions quickly and confidently &mdash; without managers losing hours every day chasing updates.

## Winning new business from existing customers: the overlooked growth strategy

When a customer already trusts your quality, pricing, and capability, the next order is usually decided by operational confidence.

Customers place repeat orders with the supplier who:

- Hits promised dates
- Flags issues early (instead of hiding them)
- Communicates clearly
- Makes progress visible

In other words: the supplier of choice.

If you become that supplier, customers don't just stay &mdash; they expand.

## On-time delivery is a sales advantage (even if you never "sell")

On-time delivery isn't just an operations KPI. It's a commercial advantage.

When you consistently deliver on time:

- Customers reduce expediting and firefighting
- Buyers feel safe placing bigger orders
- Planners stop building "supplier buffers" into lead times
- You get pulled into more projects because you're reliable

The result is simple: you win more of the work that already exists inside your accounts.

But most businesses struggle with on-time delivery for the same reason: they don't have a clean, real-time view of what's actually happening on the shop floor.

## Transparency beats perfection: how you handle "Where's my order?" matters

Even the best-run operations hit surprises: machine breakdowns, supplier delays, rework, urgent jobs.

The difference between a supplier customers trust and one they replace isn't whether problems happen &mdash; it's whether you:

- See problems early
- Communicate clearly
- Give a realistic plan
- Stick to the updated commitment

When a customer asks for a status update, they don't want a vague answer. They want:

- Where is it right now?
- What's left to do?
- When will it ship?
- What changed (if anything)?

If your team has to walk the shop, interrupt supervisors, and piece together an answer from memory, you'll lose time internally and lose confidence externally.

Fraction ERP's tracking tools are designed to make order status visible and easy to communicate.

In many manufacturing businesses, a big chunk of a production manager's day disappears into:

- Finding out what's been started

- Chasing missing information
- Updating spreadsheets
- Answering "where's my job?" questions internally
- Responding to customer enquiries

That's not management &mdash; that's expediting.

And it's expensive.
